Top Headlines

New government vs. old: the debt war escalates as Kast's State of the Union approaches

President Kast's administration has launched its biggest offensive yet against the previous Boric government, accusing former officials of misrepresenting Chile's public debt figures to Congress. Two far-right parties — the Libertarios and Republicans — filed a constitutional accusation against former Economy Minister Nicolás Grau. The move is backfiring politically: Kast's mainstream center-right coalition (Chile Vamos) is refusing to join, worried it will blow up negotiations on the government's major economic reform bill right before its first Senate vote. Former Finance Minister Briones called it "going too far" while acknowledging there may have been a real accounting error. Grau, for his part, proposed that the Boric and Kast finance teams simply sit down together.

Kast government to file first criminal complaint against Boric-era agency

After auditing the outgoing administration, the Kast government says Junaeb — a public agency that runs school lunch programs and student scholarships — will be the first institution formally referred to prosecutors. More agencies are expected to follow. It signals the new right-wing government intends to pursue actual legal proceedings, not just political scoring.

Security Minister Arrau is already under fire — and a bus was torched yesterday

New Security Minister Martín Arrau is facing growing pressure to produce a credible public safety plan. Former Interior Minister Tohá stepped in to argue that a national security framework already exists and isn't something each government builds from scratch. Meanwhile a city bus was forced to empty and set on fire at Estación Central — Arrau arrived at the scene. Crime is Chile's top domestic issue and pressure on Arrau is building fast.

San Antonio megaport finally clears environmental review after 6 years

Chile's planned major port expansion at San Antonio — the country's primary container port, roughly equivalent to Long Beach on the US West Coast — received environmental approval after six years of regulatory review. A significant win for Chile's export capacity, which is critical for a country whose economy runs on copper, lithium, and agricultural exports.

Emol

SQM profits up 191% — Chile's lithium boom is back

SQM, one of the world's largest lithium producers, reported Q1 2026 profits up 191% on a rebounding lithium price. Chile holds the world's largest lithium reserves in the Atacama desert, and the revival comes as global EV demand climbs again. Copper prices are also strong — Brent oil hit $94/barrel — making Chile's export economy look notably healthier than it did a year ago.

Elon Musk's API fees silenced Chile's earthquake alerts

Chile's national seismology service has been unable to post earthquake alerts on X since April 1 — because the platform's new API fees are too steep for a government science agency. Chile averages several significant quakes a week, making this a genuine public safety gap. They're looking for alternatives. One more story about the real-world cost of building public infrastructure on private platforms.

400+ earthquakes at Volcano Longaví — scientists watching closely

The Longaví volcano in central-south Chile has recorded over 400 earthquakes in recent days — enough for headlines. A geophysicist says it's a seismic swarm, unusual but not necessarily a sign of eruption. Scientists are monitoring. Chile sits on the Pacific Ring of Fire and has some of the world's most active volcanoes.
