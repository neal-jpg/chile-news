Economy · Congress

Kast's Reconstruction Mega-Law Clears Congress — and the Opposition Runs to Court

Chile's Chamber of Deputies on Thursday approved the final modifications to the National Reconstruction and Economic and Social Development Plan — the signature legislation of President José Antonio Kast's government — sending it to promulgation. The bill bundles a corporate income-tax cut (from 27% to 23%), a VAT exemption on new housing, 400 billion pesos ($430 million) for rebuilding fire-devastated communities in Valparaíso, Ñuble, and Biobío, and the elimination of pension contributions for workers over 65. Finance Minister Nicolás Quiroz hailed the vote, noting it passed "with broad margins, with cross-party support that goes well beyond the ruling coalition."

The opposition is not conceding. Left and center-left parties are filing constitutional challenges before Chile's Tribunal Constitucional — the equivalent of the US Supreme Court — arguing that bundling a structural tax overhaul with disaster-relief funds bypasses the proper legislative process. A handful of centrist senators broke with the left to back the bill, giving Kast the cross-aisle votes he needed. The Universal Childcare (Sala Cuna) provision was definitively stripped out during negotiations. The TC challenge could delay or alter parts of the law before Kast signs it.

Disaster

Storm Death Toll Reaches 13 — New Rain Arrives in Santiago Today

A week after the worst winter storm in decades swept Chile, the official toll stands at 13 dead, 4 missing, and more than 63,500 people still isolated across 10 of Chile's 16 regions. The Atacama and Coquimbo regions — Chile's desert north, where this amount of rain is effectively unheard of — remain under a state of catastrophe. About 150,000 homes are still without electricity. President Kast declared the emergency on July 20 and has been running daily Cabinet meetings on the response.

Today Santiago gets the tail end of the system: light to moderate rain expected through Friday morning. The Andean snowpack, meanwhile, is running 51% above the historical average for this date — a rare silver lining for a country that depends on summer melt for drinking water and agriculture. The northern road network, where many communities were cut off, is slowly being reconnected.

Politics

Kast Approval Hits Record Low: 32%

The latest Criteria survey, fielded in early July, puts President José Antonio Kast at 32% approval — a three-point drop from the previous week and the lowest number since he took office in March 2025. Disapproval stands at 55%. The sharpest slides are in economic confidence: the share of Chileans who believe Kast is "the leader Chile needs" fell eight points since May, and trust in his ability to grow the economy dropped from 54% to 48%.

The storm has not helped. The government's emergency response has drawn mixed reviews — faster than critics expected in deploying the military, but slower in meeting the 4,000-plus families who lost their homes. Chile's unemployment rate reached 9.4% in the March–May quarter, the highest in five years, and the economy showed five consecutive months of contraction before the latest data. Think of it as a president trying to pass the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act while FEMA is failing a hurricane and the unemployment rate looks like 2009.

Public Health

Surgical Wait Lists Hit All-Time Record: 458,000 Pending Operations

Chile's public health system is carrying 458,109 pending surgical procedures — a figure never previously recorded. Orthopedic and traumatology surgeries top the backlog, followed by digestive surgery and ear-nose-throat procedures. The data, released by the Health Ministry, shows a 9% year-over-year jump in the number of people waiting. The Health Minister has publicly "downplayed" the figure, pointing out that median wait times actually fell 40 days compared to last year — a statement that has drawn sharp criticism from opposition lawmakers who say the raw number of patients suffering is what matters.

Chile's two-tier health system — FONASA for public patients, ISAPRE private plans for those who can afford them — creates a persistent inequality: private patients rarely wait more than a few weeks for surgery; public patients may wait years. The record backlog is an indictment of chronic underfunding in the public tier and a political liability for an administration that campaigned on improving state services.

Interesting & Offbeat

Chile Starts Mapping the Unmapped: Landscapes That Don't Exist on Google

A Samsung-backed initiative called "Chile fuera del mapa" is inviting Chileans to photograph places that don't appear on Google Maps or Street View and upload them directly to Street View. Chile is unusually well-positioned for this: the country is home to some of the most remote and visually spectacular terrain on Earth — remote Patagonian valleys, desert altiplano, hidden coastal coves — much of which has never been formally documented online. The project uses the Galaxy S26 Ultra's camera. It's essentially a crowdsourced effort to put Chile's geography on the map, literally.

Chile's Desert North Gets Its Snowiest July in Memory — and That's Actually Good News

The same storm that killed 13 people dropped a historically unusual amount of snow on the Andes above Santiago and Coquimbo: an average of 150 centimeters across ten mountain river routes measured by the DGA (water authority) — 51% above the historical average for this date. After several drought years in central Chile that strained reservoirs and irrigation, hydrologists are cautiously optimistic: the extra snowpack could meaningfully ease summer water stress in the agricultural heartland. The catch is getting through a brutal winter to enjoy a better summer.

Opinion & Editorial
Left · The Clinic

"Employment as a National Priority"

Pamela Castro · The Clinic · July 12, 2026

Castro argues that Chile is "dangerously normalizing" a labor market emergency: 980,000 Chileans are unemployed — near the one-million mark — and fear of job loss has increased 20 percentage points. Unemployment has exceeded 8% for 41 consecutive rolling quarters with no clear path down. The editorial is a pointed challenge to the Kast government: passing a corporate tax cut is not a jobs program. The argument will resonate for anyone who remembers the Obama-era debate over whether austerity or stimulus was the right response to stubbornly high unemployment.

Center-Right · Bloomberg

"Kast Doubles Down on Tax Cuts as Chile Flirts with Recession"

Bloomberg News · July 17, 2026

Bloomberg's Chile correspondent frames the Reconstruction Law as a bet-everything moment for Kast's economic theory: if cutting corporate rates from 27% to 23% and slashing red tape can unlock investment, the economy recovers; if it doesn't, Chile will have added structural budget pressure without a growth payoff. The piece notes that Chile elected a pro-market president precisely to revive growth, but Chilean business leaders are privately saying that the reform alone won't be enough — they want permitting reform too, and the bill's permitting provisions were watered down in the Senate. No left-leaning Chilean outlet published an editorial this week that specifically debated the megarreforma's merits; the opposition's energy has gone into the TC challenge rather than the opinion pages.

Parquemet (Cerro San Cristóbal) — Free Family Activities All Month

Parquemet — the 737-hectare urban park straddling four Santiago communes and the largest urban park in Latin America — has a rolling program of free activities this month. 📊 Charts

Charts Snapshot: Urbano Dominant, Indie Scene Diverging

Chile's mainstream Spotify charts remain heavily reggaeton and urbano — trap-influenced production, collaborative features, Latin pop. The scene above is operating largely independently of those charts: psych-fusion, indie, and heavy metal carve out dedicated audiences on Bandcamp and smaller streaming playlists without competing for the Top 50. That's a healthy sign of a mature local music ecosystem where commercial and independent lanes coexist rather than one defining the other.
