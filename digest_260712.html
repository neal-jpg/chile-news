Economy · Politics

Kast Declares "Employment Emergency," Launches Subsidies as Jobless Rate Hits 5-Year High

Chile's unemployment rate climbed to 9.4% — the highest in five years — pushing President José Antonio Kast to launch "Modo Empleo" (Employment Mode) on Friday as an all-hands-on-deck government priority. The plan has three immediate legs: a four-month hiring subsidy for workers at or near minimum wage, temporary jobs through CONAF (the national forestry agency) in high-unemployment regions, and converting already-funded neighborhood improvement projects into on-the-ground jobs. Kast called the situation "concrete" and said the goal was for every ministry to work together — not to assign blame. Applications for 20,000 new SENCE employment subsidies open Tuesday, July 15.

The rollout tracks a broader pattern: Chile's monthly economic activity index (IMACEC) has now contracted five straight months year-over-year, and the Central Bank has cut its 2026 GDP forecast to 1.0–1.75%. The Kast administration, just four months in office, is under growing pressure — his latest Cadem poll puts disapproval at 60%, the highest since he took La Moneda in March.

Environment · Today

Alerta Ambiental in Effect for Santiago Right Now — Wood Heaters Banned

Chile's Environment Ministry declared an Environmental Alert (Alerta Ambiental) for all of the Metropolitan Region for Sunday, citing poor atmospheric ventilation and elevated PM2.5 particulate matter. This is the third alert episode in roughly a week — prior alerts covered Friday July 10 and around July 8 as well. Under the alert, all wood-burning heaters are prohibited; pellet stoves are permitted. No vehicle circulation restrictions apply on weekends. Forecasters blame a weak anticyclonic pressure system with no coastal breeze reaching the basin.

Politics · Economy

Kast's Economic Mega-Reform Heads to Full Senate Vote Wednesday After Winning Over Opposition Senators

The government's sweeping economic reform package — the "National Reconstruction Law," nicknamed the megarreforma — is set for a full Senate chamber vote Wednesday, July 15, after weeks of committee negotiations. The government reformulated a key business training tax credit (the SENCE Franquicia Tributaria), cutting it in half for large firms, which brought over votes from Communist and Socialist senators. A center-left PPD party cut a separate deal on a phased tax stability clause for big investments. The reform package would gradually cut Chile's corporate tax from 27% to 23%, offer a temporary VAT exemption for new home sales, and streamline permitting to unlock stalled construction projects. Opposition calls it a corporate giveaway; the government says it's the only path to reactivating a contracting economy. The vote is also shadowed by the ongoing desafuero proceedings against independent senator Miguel Ángel Calisto, whose vote is essential to the government's thin Senate majority.

Politics · Government

Ministry of Science Hit by Second Wave of Layoffs; Workers Say Trust with Minister "Has Broken"

Chile's Ministry of Science, Technology, Knowledge and Innovation saw a second round of dismissals this week — at least 11–12 workers let go over two days including two department heads, an IT manager, and communications staff. The National Association of Ministry Workers (ANFUCYT) says the relationship with Minister Ximena Lincolao has fractured irreparably. The conflict traces to May, when Undersecretary Rafael Araos publicly resigned after refusing to execute what he described as an order to dismiss up to 48 workers — a charge the minister denied. The ministry says most recent departures were voluntary resignations with compensated vacations. Think of it as a bureaucratic purge fight playing out inside a cabinet agency — analogous to a new US administration cleaning house at an agency like the NSF, but with the fired staff going public about how it happened.

Foreign Policy

Chile Abstains on UN Cuba Embargo Vote — Quietly Aligning with US and Argentina

When the UN General Assembly voted 136–8 to open an emergency debate on the US economic embargo against Cuba, Chile abstained — a significant departure from the Boric government, which had voted in favor. Chile's ambassador Roberto Ampuero justified the abstention by saying the issue is too "binary" and that Cuba's situation will only improve when its citizens can freely choose their political path. The move signals a sharp foreign-policy realignment: Chile now votes alongside the United States, Israel, and Argentina on Cuba-related matters. For Kast, the right-wing president who won on an explicitly anti-left platform, it's a deliberate signal to his base — and to Washington — about where Chile's new foreign policy priorities lie.

Interesting & Offbeat

Chile's SUCHAI-4 Nanosatellite Is Now Orbiting Earth After SpaceX Rideshare

A 5-kilogram CubeSat built entirely by the Universidad de Chile's Space Exploration Lab (SPEL) hitched a ride on SpaceX's Transporter-17 rideshare mission from Vandenberg Air Force Base, California on July 7 — and is now circling Earth at roughly 590 km altitude in polar orbit. It will run about 60 experiments, including Chile's first test of laser-based optical communications (faster and more secure than radio), a low-power radio system for reaching remote regions, UV and infrared telescopy, and biological samples placed in the space environment. Five Chilean universities collaborated on the project alongside international partners. The SUCHAI program (Universidad de Chile Satellite) has been running for over a decade, making Chile one of the few countries in Latin America to develop and launch its own satellite technology domestically.

El Mostrador / País Circular · Center-Green · Today

"Chile Eliminated Plastic Bags — Now It Must Eliminate Green Lies"

Marking eight years since Chile banned single-use plastic bags (Ley 21.100, "Chao Bolsas Plásticas"), this op-ed praises the law — 11.5 billion fewer bags entering landfills, rivers, and the ocean — while naming the next battle: greenwashing. A flood of bags now marketed as "biodegradable," "compostable," or "eco-friendly" often fail to deliver on those claims because verification standards are weak and enforcement weaker. The author (a waste-industry sustainability manager) calls for stricter labeling rules and third-party certification before any bag can carry an environmental claim. Lean: technocratic green; not a left/right piece.

Rock

Los Tres — XCLNT

Released April 10, 2026, *XCLNT* is the first studio album from Los Tres' original "La Revuelta" lineup in 26 years — recorded at Abbey Road Studios in London and still generating critical conversation three months in. The album debuted to wide critical acclaim and became the most-streamed Chilean rock release in years. Lead single "Cantar y Amar" (released March 2026) was the first original material from this lineup since the late '90s. If you know Los Tres as the most important Chilean rock band of the '90s — think Radiohead-meets-Chilean-folklore in terms of cultural weight — this is the equivalent of a 26-year-later reunion album recorded at the most famous studio on earth. For fans of: alt-rock, Latin rock, La Ley, Javiera Mena's roots.
