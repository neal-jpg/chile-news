Politics

Coalition Friction: RN Puts Formal Demands on Kast's Desk

Chile's ruling coalition hit turbulence as Renovación Nacional (RN) — the largest moderate-right party, roughly the GOP's mainstream wing — met President Kast at his Cerro Castillo coastal retreat and delivered a formal list of demands: a system to resolve intra-coalition disputes within 48 hours, weekly political committee meetings, more transparency, and greater influence over the legislative agenda.

The tension is an old story in right-wing Chilean politics. Kast's Partido Republicano (the hard-right newcomers, think MAGA-adjacent) has repeatedly accused RN of being too soft and too independent, while RN says it won't be taken for granted by its smaller coalition partners. Kast tried to lower the temperature at the meeting, calling for "afiatar las confianzas" (trust-building). Whether it sticks is another matter.

National Emergency

South Battered by Historic Storms — Santiago Next in Line

The government declared a preventive emergency across 10 regions — from Atacama in the north to Los Lagos in the south — running July 13–21, as a severe multi-threat frontal system sweeps the country. The south already took the worst of it: 107 homes damaged, 84 people displaced. The fishing town of Corral (Los Ríos) recorded 300mm of rainfall in just a few days — more than the entire previous month of July.

This is Kast's first major climate emergency response, and he ordered cabinet ministers with regional portfolios to deploy on the ground. Senapred (Chile's FEMA equivalent) has yellow alerts active across southern regions. Santiago should expect rain and cold moving in from mid-week through the weekend.

Economy

"Modo Empleo" Goes Live Today as Unemployment Hits 9.4%

Chile is one month away from an official recession — five consecutive months of economic contraction (think of it like negative GDP quarters in the US) — and unemployment just hit 9.4%, the highest in five years, with nearly a million Chileans out of work. Today the Kast government officially launches "Modo Empleo" (Employment Mode): 20,000 new hiring subsidies open for applications. The government covers 50% of the minimum wage per new hire, bumped to 60% for women. A 50 billion-peso ($55M) inter-ministerial fund backs the package, with a target of 50,000 new jobs by October.

Critics, especially on the left, argue subsidies alone can't fix structural labor market problems. Kast himself acknowledged last month that Chile faces a "fairly critical" six months ahead on the economy.

Security & Economy

Business Elite Sounds Alarm: Criminal Markets Moving $5.7B a Year

The CPC — Chile's peak business federation, the rough equivalent of the US Chamber of Commerce — released a landmark report: illegal markets now move US$5.7 billion per year. Biggest drivers include illegal online casinos ($2B), counterfeit goods, fish theft, and contraband across 45 identified categories. The report calls it a "structural threat" to Chile's formal economy, not just a security problem.

The government responded by announcing a "Registry of Vandals and Incivilities" bill — a narrower measure than the sweeping anti-organized crime response business groups are calling for. The gap between the scale of the problem and the policy response is drawing criticism from across the political spectrum.

Ice on Ice: Parque Araucano's Outdoor Rink Is Peak Winter Holiday

With Santiago kids on vacaciones de invierno, the outdoor ice rink at Parque Araucano (Las Condes) is in full swing. A 700m² sheet of ice with rental skates and helmets, open Tuesday–Sunday 10am–9pm, Monday 2pm–9pm — 60 minutes per session. It's quintessentially Chilean: an entirely incongruous bit of alpine infrastructure in a Mediterranean-climate city that almost never sees actual snow. Afternoon slots are selling out during holiday week. If you haven't taken the kids yet, this afternoon is the weather window.

"El espejismo del empleo: lecciones desde La Araucanía"

Published today in El Mostrador, Chile's main center-left news site: the columnist argues that the government's employment figures mask a harsher reality — seasonal, precarious, and informal work inflating headline numbers while stable formal jobs remain out of reach for many Chileans, particularly outside Santiago. La Araucanía is used as a case study where official metrics look better than lived experience. A useful counterweight to today's "Modo Empleo" launch fanfare.

La Tercera · Center-right

"Empleo y seguridad económica: dos caras de la misma moneda"

La Tercera's opinion desk argues that economic insecurity — job loss, unpaid bills, an uncertain future — has overtaken physical crime as Chileans' number-one fear. That's a significant shift for a country that spent years laser-focused on street violence and organized gangs. The piece urges Kast to treat the employment crisis with the same urgency he brought to security, noting that voters who elected him on a law-and-order platform are now worried primarily about keeping their jobs.

🍷 Wine

Expo CAV 2026 — Chilean Wine Festival · July 23–25 · Espacio Riesco

One of Santiago's signature winter gastronomic events: more than 50 Chilean wineries from across the country's valleys descend on Espacio Riesco (Las Condes) for three days of tastings, masterclasses, food pairings, and serious drinking. Chile is the world's largest exporter of Carménère — a grape practically extinct in its home region of Bordeaux — and this is one of the best places to drink your way through the country's wine geography. Tickets on PuntoTicket.
