Top Headlines
Weather Emergency

Chile braces for biggest winter storm in years as Kast deploys military and rain arrives in Santiago this afternoon

An "atmospheric river" — a narrow corridor of concentrated moisture in the upper atmosphere — is funneling back-to-back frontal systems into Chile, threatening up to 180 millimeters of rain over five days in the Santiago metropolitan area. That would rank among the worst winter events in decades for the capital. The government declared a Preventive Emergency from Atacama to Los Ríos (10 of 16 regions), placed the Armed Forces on standby for evacuations, and suspended Friday classes across five regions. Santiago starts seeing rain this afternoon, with the worst window expected Thursday night through Sunday.

President Kast canceled his travel schedule and visited the Navy's hydrological service in Valparaíso to monitor conditions in person — framing this as "a state matter, not just a government matter," and calling on citizens to prepare emergency kits and check on neighbors. It is his first major climate emergency since taking office in March, and an early test of his government's disaster-response credibility. SENAPRED (Chile's FEMA equivalent) identified 2,363 high-risk points across the country; the Armed Forces are quartered for rapid deployment.

Justice

Fugitive ex-mayor Karen Rojo arrives in Chile in handcuffs, ending a four-year extradition saga

Karen Rojo, the former mayor of Antofagasta who fled Chile the day after the Supreme Court rejected her final appeal in March 2022, landed at Santiago's airport Wednesday escorted by PDI detectives — ending four years as a fugitive in the Netherlands. She was convicted of "fraude al fisco": using roughly 23 million pesos (~$25,000 USD) in city funds to pay a political communications firm that worked on her image during her 2016 reelection campaign. Think a city mayor quietly routing the parks budget to her own campaign consultants.

Her sentence is five years of effective imprisonment. Her defense argues she has already served about 80% of it during her Dutch detention and hopes a Chilean court will grant supervised release. The case will be reviewed by a guarantee court in Antofagasta in the coming weeks. Her arrival in handcuffs is a rare, visible accountability moment in Chilean politics — proof that officials who flee the country still face eventual justice.

Economy

IMF cuts Chile's 2026 growth to 1.8% as fifth straight monthly contraction raises recession alarm

Chile's IMACEC — the monthly economic indicator that functions like a monthly GDP reading — fell 0.9% in May, its fifth consecutive decline. Two negative quarters back-to-back would formally meet the definition of a technical recession. The IMF has now cut its Chile growth forecast twice this year, to 1.8%; the Banco Central is gloomier still at 1.3%. Inflation remains stuck at 4.3% — above the 3% target — and unemployment sits at 9.4%, leaving the government boxed in: stimulus risks inflation, austerity risks deeper contraction.

Exports to the United States fell 34% in the first five months of 2026, a direct consequence of ongoing tariff pressure, and copper prices haven't recovered enough to offset the trade hit. The Kast government has wagered on fiscal discipline and a copper rebound — but the latest numbers suggest the year may be close to already "played out," as the CPC (Chile's peak business association) put it, with growth unlikely to top 1.5% for the full year.

Public Health

Chile now has 91,000 people living with HIV — 86% more than a decade ago — and barely talks about it

New analysis published ahead of World AIDS Day shows Chile's HIV caseload has surged 86% over ten years, reaching 91,000 people — making it one of Latin America's fastest-growing epidemics in proportional terms. The country performs well on the medical metrics: it achieves a 95% diagnosis rate and 95% viral suppression among people in treatment, numbers that rival the United States. But only 75% of those who need antiretrovirals are actually receiving them, and access to PrEP (a daily preventive pill that stops HIV transmission) remains limited and poorly publicized.

Health experts say the core gap is cultural: HIV stigma runs deep, sex education in schools is thin, and the topic rarely surfaces in mainstream political debate. A Cooperativa columnist compared Chile's HIV silence to how the U.S. handled the opioid crisis in its early years — a well-understood public health failure that exists largely outside the political conversation.

Interesting / Offbeat

The giant storm dumping on Santiago might be the best thing for your lungs all year

Santiago sits in a bowl-shaped valley ringed by the Andes and coastal mountains, trapping winter air and cooking up some of Latin America's worst seasonal smog. On the worst days this winter the city's particulate count has hit "unhealthy" levels, and SEREMI de Salud has issued multiple air-quality alerts banning wood fires. The "atmospheric river" arriving today is the fastest route to relief: rain scrubs fine particulates from the air, and the windy days that follow a major front flush the valley entirely. So while the flooding risk is real, the city's air is likely to be noticeably cleaner by the weekend. Silver lining: the very thing flooding basements is also clearing lungs. Expect an AQI drop of 30–50 points by Friday evening, weather permitting.

A BioBioChile economic analysis argues that even taking the Kast government at its word on spending discipline, Chile's fiscal deficit will remain above 2% of GDP through 2026 and the budget margins will stay negative through at least 2030. Album of the Winter
Rock Indie

Los Tres — XCLNT

Chile's most important rock band dropped their first album with the original lineup in 26 years back in April, and it has been the conversation of the Chilean music year ever since. XCLNT was recorded at Abbey Road — yes, that Abbey Road — with the three original members (Álvaro Henríquez, Titae Lindl, and Roberto "Florcita" Morales) reunited after decades of solo careers. The lead single "Cantar y Amar," released in March, showcases the band's signature blend of Chilean folk tonalities and driving rock arrangements. For fans of: Pearl Jam meeting Los Jaivas, or the more muscular side of R.E.M. If you only listen to one Chilean record this year, make it this one.

Marineros — "La esquina de los sueños rotos"

The Chilean indie duo Marineros released this new single in the past two weeks, and it's quietly one of the most polished things to come out of the independent scene this winter. Hip Hop

Kidd Voodoo — Euforia

Released in May, this eight-track debut album from the Santiago rapper Kidd Voodoo has been picking up streams steadily through the winter. What makes it interesting: the features. Mon Laferte, Pablo Alborán, and Spanish rapper Rels B appear alongside Voodoo, creating an unusual cross-genre, cross-border record that sits somewhere between melodic trap, pop, and urban R&B. It's been called Chile's first serious attempt at "global Spanish-language rap" — drawing comparisons to Bad Bunny's crossover period but with a distinctly Chilean vocal register. Worth checking if you're curious about where Chilean hip hop is heading.
