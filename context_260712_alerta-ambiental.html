Santiago's Air Quality Alert System

Santiago sits in a valley ringed by the Andes to the east and a coastal mountain range to the west. In winter, cold air settles into the basin and traps pollution close to the ground — a thermal inversion that prevents smog from dispersing. The main culprit is PM2.5, fine particulate matter from wood-burning heaters (still common in hundreds of thousands of Santiago homes), diesel vehicles, and industrial sources. On bad days the Andes vanish behind a brownish haze.

To manage these episodes, Chile's Ministry of the Environment (MMA) declares one of three escalating alerts for the Metropolitan Region when overnight PM2.5 forecasts look bad. Think of it as Santiago's version of the US Air Quality Index (AQI) color scale — except with specific legal restrictions attached:

Alerta Ambiental (Environmental Alert) — roughly AQI 100–150. Wood-burning heaters are banned throughout the region (certified pellet stoves may still be used). No vehicle circulation restrictions apply on weekends. Outdoors you might notice a milky-grey haze; the Andes are blurry or invisible. Sensitive groups — children, the elderly, people with asthma — are advised to limit outdoor exertion.

Preemergencia Ambiental (Pre-Emergency) — roughly AQI 150–200. Heaters still banned, and on weekdays about 20% of vehicles (determined by license-plate number) cannot circulate, including those with catalytic converters. This is the level where you start seeing noticeably dark smog and the air has a faint acrid smell.

Emergencia Ambiental (Emergency) — AQI 200+. The rarest tier. Wood heaters banned, roughly 40% of vehicles restricted, and certain industrial processes must halt. Health authorities issue strong advisories to stay indoors.

The analogy for Americans: California's Bay Area Air Quality Management District issues "Spare the Air Alerts" that ban wood fires and warn residents to limit outdoor activity — same concept, slightly different enforcement. On the East Coast, "Code Orange" or "Code Red" air days on the AQI scale prompt similar advisories.

Today, July 12, 2026 — Alerta Ambiental is in effect. Wood-burning heaters are prohibited. AQI readings in eastern Santiago (Las Condes, Providencia) are running in the 100–130 range this morning. Keep windows closed if you're sensitive to air quality, and skip the outdoor run. The Andes are not visible today.
