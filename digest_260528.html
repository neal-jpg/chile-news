Opposition Moves to Impeach Economy Minister — and the Right Is Eating Itself Over It

The Republican Party and its libertarian allies filed a acusación constitucional — Chile's version of congressional impeachment — against Economy Minister Nicolás Grau, targeting his handling of the government's flagship mega tax-and-investment reform. But the move is fracturing the opposition: Chile Vamos, the center-right coalition supposed to be aligned with the Republicans, is visibly uncomfortable. Senator Squella tried to put out the fire, saying "there's no appetite to pick a fight" and urging focus on what unites them. Ruling coalition figures, meanwhile, allege the whole operation is secretly coordinated with the government — making it political theater. The accusation threatens to poison ongoing negotiations over the megarreforma at exactly the wrong moment.

Business / Courts

"Cousin Collusion" — Meal Voucher Execs Face Prison Time

Chile's antitrust authority (FNE — think the FTC) pushed criminal charges against executives at Pluxee and Edenred for allegedly fixing prices and dividing the meal-voucher market. The case is nicknamed la colusión de los primos because the companies' top executives are cousins. Some now face actual jail time, not just civil fines — relatively rare in Chilean competition law.

🏥 Air Force Hospital Closes Its Maternity Ward — Birth Rate Is to Blame

Santiago's Hospital FACh, the Air Force's flagship hospital serving the city's eastern sector, is shutting down its historic maternity ward. The reason isn't budget cuts — it's Chile's birth rate crisis. There simply aren't enough babies being born to justify keeping it open. Chile's fertility rate has dropped sharply over the past decade, and this closure is one of the first concrete institutional casualties. Think of it like a small-town hospital closing its maternity floor because the town stopped having kids.

📱 "Burn La Moneda" Posts Prompt Police Alert Near Plaza Dignidad

Social media accounts — some linked to Mapuche activist networks — have been posting nostalgic content about the 2019 social uprising alongside calls to gather at Plaza Dignidad (formerly Plaza Italia, the symbolic center of those protests). Some posts include phrases like "burn La Moneda," Chile's presidential palace. Police are on alert and officials are calling for investigations into platforms spreading information about potential attacks. Nothing has materialized, but the language has security services watching closely.
