Top Headlines
Politics

Kast's approval hits all-time low as unemployment anxiety mounts

Two polls published this weekend put President José Antonio Kast at the lowest approval of his young presidency. The Criteria survey released Sunday shows 32% approval and 55% disapproval — a new floor since he took office in March. The weekly Cadem Plaza Pública (released Saturday) registers 38% approval and 58% disapproval, down three points week-over-week. Both polls point to the same driver: the economy, particularly a 9.4% unemployment rate — the highest in five years.

The sharpest drop inside Criteria's data came in the attribute "Is the leader Chile needs," which slid from 42% to 34% in a single week. Kast's base (those who voted for him in the first round) still approves at over 70%, suggesting a core-versus-periphery dynamic familiar from US politics — think Trump's base staying loyal through economic turbulence while independents sour.

Economy

"Modo Empleo" — Kast's plan to create 50,000 jobs in four months

Facing a 9.4% unemployment rate (the highest since 2021), Kast launched "Modo Empleo" (Employment Mode) on July 10 — a declaration that job creation is now a whole-of-government priority, not just the Labor Ministry's problem. Three concrete tracks: a hiring subsidy covering 50% of the minimum wage for four months (60% when the hire is a woman), temporary jobs through CONAF (Chile's national forestry agency) in high-unemployment regions, and fast-tracking already-approved neighborhood improvement projects. The goal is 50,000 new formal jobs in four months, funded with $50 billion pesos.

Youth unemployment is especially ugly at 22.8%, reaching 28% for young women. Economists are skeptical the measures are large enough, but the political framing — "we're not looking for who to blame, we have a concrete situation and everyone needs to work on it" (Kast's words) — is a clear attempt to shift from crisis mode to action mode ahead of worsening polls.

Health

Senate unanimously rejects health budget cuts; workers warn of system breakdown

The government's $413 billion peso cut to the Health Ministry (about 2.5% of its annual budget) has run into unified resistance. The Senate's health commission voted unanimously to reject the reduction, calling it a real-world threat to patients. The cuts — authorized under a Hacienda decree — slice 0.9% from regional health services, 0.5% from primary care clinics, and 1.1% from mid- and high-complexity hospitals.

FENATS, the largest public-sector health workers' union, says the result is already visible: frozen hiring, suspended equipment purchases, and teams operating at the edge. The political dynamic here is similar to US debates over Medicaid cuts — the government says the savings are manageable, providers say the math doesn't work on the ground. Kast's health team has not budged publicly.

Congress

Reconstruction law in final stretch — Senate Finance votes on 365 amendments

The major earthquake reconstruction bill took another step forward Friday when the Senate Finance Commission worked through a fresh batch of the 365 proposed amendments. UDI president Guillermo Ramírez says he expects the law to pass before the end of July. The bill creates a special fast-track process for disaster victims to obtain property titles — clearing the bureaucratic blockage that stops many families from qualifying for housing subsidies.

Left · Column

El Mostrador · July 13, 2026

"La emergencia real que se vive en Chile y el papel de la izquierda en su superación"

Published today on El Mostrador (Chile's leading center-left digital outlet), this column argues that Chile's real crisis is economic and social — not ideological — and challenges the left to offer a credible, constructive alternative to Kast's administration rather than simply celebrating its declining poll numbers. The author notes that Kast entered office in March with 57% approval per Cadem and is now at around 37%, driven by the same unemployment crisis battering his Modo Empleo plan. The piece reads as a call for the left to graduate from opposition to governance-ready — roughly the challenge the Democrats faced in 2009 when they had to do more than criticize the financial crisis.

Right · Column

El Líbero · Recent

"Chile en emergencia económica"

A right-leaning economic post-mortem from El Líbero, Chile's leading conservative digital outlet. The column pins the current employment crisis squarely on the Boric government's labor reforms: a 30% minimum-wage hike without matching productivity gains, reduced working hours at full pay, and a six-point pension contribution increase that raised employer costs by roughly 60%. The argument — familiar from supply-side economics — is that making labor expensive is what froze hiring. The current 9.4% unemployment, the writer contends, is the predictable result. Critics on the left dispute this framing, arguing weak global demand and the post-earthquake economic shock are the real culprits.

Rock

Los Tres — "XCLNT"

Released in April but still dominating conversations about Chilean rock — XCLNT is Los Tres' first studio album with their original lineup in 27 years. Recorded at Abbey Road in London, the 11-track record reunites Álvaro Henríquez, Pancho Molina, Ángel Parra Jr., and Roberto "Titae" Lindl and delivers the kind of melodic, slightly rough-edged Chilean alternative rock that made them legends in the '90s. Singles "Cantar y amar" and "Como llegaste te vas" have been streaming staples. If you haven't given it a full listen yet, a rainy winter Monday is the right moment.

Electronic

Lizz (Elisa Espinoza) — "PERRAS CORE"

Santiago DJ, producer, and experimentadora Lizz dropped this 11-track album in March and it keeps circulating in the city's electronic scene. The record is an unapologetic Latin club mixtape — neoperreo at maximum intensity — with collaborators including King Dou Dou, La Goony, Genosidra, and DJ Sustancia. It's a genre portrait (neo-perreo/Latin electronic) rather than a live-show record; the production craft rewards headphone listening even if the floor is where it really lives. For fans of: Tomasa del Real, Agorazein, anything out of Chile's club underground.
