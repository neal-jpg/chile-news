Economy · Politics

Megarreforma Clears Finance Committee — Senate Floor Vote Set for Wednesday as PPD Deal Cracks the Opposition

Kast's sweeping economic reform package — his presidency's defining legislation — is now heading to a full Senate floor vote scheduled for Wednesday July 15, after the Finance Committee wrapped up its review of amendments. The package cuts the corporate tax rate from 27% to 23% over several years (roughly the Chilean version of the 2017 US TCJA corporate rate cut), adds a temporary VAT exemption on new-home purchases, provides investment stability guarantees, and includes a senior property-tax relief measure. It passed out of the Environment Committee last week despite the walkout of opposition senators.

The PPD (a centrist opposition party, roughly center-left Democrats) struck a last-minute deal with Finance Minister Jorge Quiroz on the most contested element — "tax invariability," a guarantee that investors' rates won't change for years. The renegotiated version scales the period: 10 years for investments up to $100M, 15 years up to $350M, and 20 years above that, down from a flat 25. The PPD's Senate caucus won't take that provision to the Constitutional Court (TC) — Chile's version of the Supreme Court — and its four votes are crucial. But the PPD's national leadership publicly overruled its own senators and is still pursuing the TC challenge, as is the Socialist Party. Former President Eduardo Frei (DC, Christian Democrats) came out strongly defending the deal Thursday, saying Chile isn't "jumping into an empty pool" — a direct rebuke of his own coalition partners.

Politics · Courts

Senator Calisto Desafuero Hearing Paused — His Vote Could Be the Deciding Factor

The Puerto Montt Court of Appeals suspended the desafuero hearing for Senator Miguel Ángel Calisto (independent, pro-government) after prosecutors reopened the fraud-against-the-state investigation against him. The procedural pause means his congressional immunity remains in place for now — but the situation creates a cloud over the megarreforma vote. Calisto's single vote is essential to the government's razor-thin Senate majority; if he's eventually suspended or absent due to a medical leave he's currently on, no substitute fills his seat (independents outside a party pact don't get replacements). Senate President Núñez said publicly that, ethically, Calisto should recuse himself from the megarreforma vote given the circumstances — an unusual request that highlights how high the stakes are.

Economy

Five Straight Monthly Declines: Chile Flirting with Recession as Unemployment Hits 9.4%

Chile's IMACEC — the monthly economic activity index, roughly the Chilean equivalent of monthly GDP — fell 0.9% in May compared with the same month last year, marking the fifth consecutive monthly decline. The unemployment rate hit 9.4%, the highest in five years, with about 950,000 Chileans out of work. President Kast acknowledged the "complex scenario" while Finance Minister Quiroz insisted there are "no signs of recession," pointing to preliminary June indicators he says show a turning point. The Cadem poll released this week found 52% of Chileans believe the economic situation has gotten worse recently, and Kast's approval slipped to 38% — its lowest since he took office.

The megarreforma is the government's primary response: cut corporate taxes, incentivize investment, and reduce permitting burdens to kickstart growth. Critics on the left say the bill is a giveaway to the wealthy that will cost the treasury over $4 billion a year in lost revenue. Supporters say Chile needs a business-environment reset after years of regulatory uncertainty. Think of it as a supply-side debate similar to the US argument over the Bush or Trump tax cuts — does cutting corporate rates generate enough growth to offset the revenue hit?

Politics

"Voluntad para Chile": A New Left Party Begins National Tour This Weekend

Valparaíso Governor Rodrigo Mundaca and figures including former senators and local leaders are consolidating a new left-wing political movement called "Voluntad para Chile" (Will for Chile). This weekend they launch a national organizing tour in Concepción — Chile's third-largest city — to build toward formally registering as a political party. The movement includes prominent figures like Fabiola Campillai (a senator who was blinded by a tear-gas canister during the 2019 protests, roughly Chile's most high-profile protest-era political figure) and sees itself as a force to the left of the existing Frente Amplio and Communist Party blocs. Think of it as a new progressive party forming in the space between the Squad-style left and more traditional social democracy — at a moment when the governing right is trying to push through its economic agenda and the current left-center opposition has fractured over the megarreforma.

Interesting & Offbeat

Customs Busted a $10 Million Coin Smuggling Ring — Hidden in Scrap Metal Headed to China

Chilean customs officers at the port of Valparaíso intercepted an unusual shipment: more than $10 million pesos in 10-peso coins packed inside containers labeled as scrap metal, bound for China. The 10-peso coin is the smallest denomination in circulation — worth about one US cent — but large quantities of them are apparently valuable as raw copper-and-aluminum alloy to recyclers abroad. The export of coins is illegal in Chile without central bank authorization. It's the most elaborate coin-smuggling scheme Customs says it has seen in years, and investigators suspect it's part of a broader metal recycling scam targeting the mint's coin stock.

El Mostrador · Left · July 8

"Not Just One Office Less: 100 Days of a State Walking Back on Human Rights"

Columnist Claudia Heiss argues that the Kast government's closure of UBAFI — the unit created to find victims of illegal, forced, and irregular adoptions that took place under the Pinochet dictatorship — is not an administrative trimming but a deliberate ideological choice. She counts a pattern: shuttering human rights offices, cutting ombudsman budgets, and rolling back oversight bodies that documented state abuses. For an American analogy, think of it as defunding the Truth and Reconciliation offices: the offices themselves may be small, but what they represent — official acknowledgment of past wrongs — is why their closure carries symbolic weight far beyond their budgets. The right-of-center government sees much of this infrastructure as politicized; the left sees its removal as erasure.

"We're Not Jumping Into an Empty Pool" — Frei's Defense of the PPD Deal

Former President Eduardo Frei Ruiz-Tagle (Christian Democrats, 1994–2000) broke with his coalition's dominant line Thursday and endorsed the PPD's decision to cut a deal with Hacienda on tax invariability rather than run to the courts. His argument: Chile is in a genuine economic emergency — five months of IMACEC declines, 9.4% unemployment — and blocking investment guarantees on procedural grounds would hurt workers more than corporations. The column drew sharp replies from PS and Frente Amplio legislators who said he was lending cover to a bill that widens inequality. It also underlined how fragmented the center-left has become: the same coalition that governed Chile for decades is now publicly arguing over whether to help Kast's signature bill pass.

Rock Indie

Candelabro — Deseo, Carne y Voluntad

The biggest story in Chilean rock right now: Candelabro swept the Premios Pulsar 2026 in June, winning both Album of the Year and Best Rock Album for their second LP Deseo, Carne y Voluntad. The album weaves spiritual and political language into a dense, heavy sound — reviewers called it "a ferocious demonstration of musical maturity." Their Lollapalooza set in March was a breakthrough moment for a band that had been a scene favorite without mainstream crossover; the Pulsar double win cemented them as the most discussed Chilean rock act of the year. For fans of: early Radiohead meets Bauhaus with Latin American political poetry. Essential if you haven't heard it yet.

Gepe & Cancamusa — Dopamina

Gepe (Daniel Riveros) is Chile's most beloved indie-folk craftsman — think of him as the Chilean equivalent of Sufjan Stevens crossed with Caetano Veloso. His collaboration single "Dopamina" with singer-drummer Cancamusa won Song of the Year at the Pulsars, and their live rendition at Lollapalooza in March was widely cited as one of the best moments of the festival. The single is understated and warm; if you don't know Gepe yet, start here.
