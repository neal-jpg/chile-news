Politics · Economy

The "Mega-Reform" Is Cracking — From Every Direction

Chile's biggest legislative fight of the year took several punches at once. The right-wing Republican Party filed a constitutional accusation — think impeachment proceedings in the Senate — against ex-Finance Minister Mario Grau, the former chief architect of the so-called "Megarreforma," a sweeping mining investment package. Separately, the Kast government admitted the reform needs significant changes after the Finance Ministry's public finances report flagged inconsistencies. The most notable concession: cutting the proposed tax stability guarantee from 25 years down to 20. The government set July as the target to advance the bill, and the center-left Socialist Party, the opposition's largest Senate bloc, will be the deciding vote. One rival faction — the Libertarians — has already accused the government and Republicans of coordinating the impeachment move to derail the reform rather than strengthen it.

International

Bachelet Courts World Leaders in Her UN Secretary-General Bid

Former President Michelle Bachelet met with Britain's Foreign Secretary in London and is awaiting a meeting with French President Macron as part of her campaign to lead the United Nations. She is one of the frontrunners to replace current Secretary-General António Guterres, whose term ends at the end of 2026. If elected, she would be the first Latin American and one of the very few women ever to hold the role. Back home, her candidacy has drawn praise across the political spectrum — even Kast's government allies have said her run "transcends ideology."

Generic Ozempic Is Almost Here for Chilean Patients

Chile's drug regulator (ISP — similar to the FDA) is on the verge of approving the first generic version of semaglutide for treating diabetes and obesity. Brand-name semaglutide (sold as Ozempic and Wegovy) is widely used but expensive. A generic version would dramatically lower costs for the hundreds of thousands of Chileans managing these conditions. No approval date has been announced, but officials say it is imminent.

Carabineros Shoot and Kill Taxi Driver After He Rammed an Officer

Police shot and killed a taxi driver in the Recoleta district of Santiago early Thursday after he evaded a checkpoint and then accelerated his vehicle toward an officer. The incident is the latest in a series of high-profile police force incidents that have reignited debate about Carabineros' protocols for lethal force. In a separate incident in Chiguayante (near Concepción), a driver dragged an officer for over a kilometer before being arrested.

Business Groups Push Back on Crackdown Targeting Companies With Undocumented Workers

Chile's immigration authority has begun inspecting businesses suspected of employing undocumented migrants and signaled it will intensify controls. Business associations are pushing back, warning about labor shortages and potential disruption to sectors — particularly agriculture and construction — that depend heavily on migrant labor. The government argues enforcement is consistent with the law. La Tercera's editorial board weighed in on the broader controversy around deportations this week.

🌋 Volcano Longaví Has Recorded 400+ Earthquakes. Should You Worry?

Chile's Longaví volcano, located in the Andes about 400 km south of Santiago, has registered over 400 seismic events in recent days. Geophysicists say this level of activity — while worth watching — is consistent with the normal "breathing" of an active volcanic system and does not indicate an imminent eruption. Still, Chilean authorities have monitoring systems in place, and this kind of cluster always gets attention in a country that sits squarely on the Pacific Ring of Fire.
