Politics

Kast Approval Sinks to Historic Low of 32% as Disapproval Hits 55%

The July Criteria poll — Chile's most-watched monthly approval survey, roughly analogous to a Gallup presidential tracker in the U.S. — put President José Antonio Kast at just 32% approval, down three points in a week and the lowest reading of his administration since he took office in March. Disapproval reached 55%. The steepest slide is in the attribute "the leader Chile needs," which has fallen eight points in two months to 34% — a significant erosion of the personal-brand case he made in the campaign.

All four key leadership metrics declined simultaneously: economic management (down to 48%), crime-fighting credibility (down to 39%), public security (down to 42%), and leadership quality. Analysts connect the drop to frustrations over a stalled legislative agenda, a foreign-policy controversy (see Opinion section), and a worsening healthcare crisis that has dominated the last two weeks of headlines. The administration says it is laying long-term groundwork and points to structural reforms still in process.

Health

400,000 Chileans Waiting for Surgery — an All-Time Record. The Health Minister Says Don't Panic.

Chile's public health network hit a grim milestone this week: 458,109 pending surgical interventions for 398,496 patients, the worst backlog in the history of the system. The total healthcare waiting list — counting specialist consultations and ongoing treatments — stands at 2.5 million people. Orthopedic, digestive, and dermatological cases account for nearly half the surgical queue. Health Minister Chomalí has downplayed the numbers, noting that the median wait time actually dropped by 40 days (to 259 days) compared to a year ago.

For context on why this matters so much: Chile's public insurer, Fonasa, covers roughly 80% of the population — think of it as a mandatory Medicaid funded by a 7% payroll deduction. When Fonasa queues run this long, lower-income families effectively lose access to elective care. A new CEP think-tank report out today warns the crisis is deeper than the headline backlog (see next story).

Health · Analysis

Think-Tank Report: Chile's Real Health Emergency Is Cancer Gaps and Sky-High Out-of-Pocket Costs

A major joint study released today by the Centro de Estudios Públicos (CEP — Chile's equivalent of the Brookings Institution) and CIPS warns that the surgical backlog is only the visible tip of the iceberg. Two deeper crises: Chile's cancer care infrastructure is under-resourced relative to the country's incidence rates, and 35% of all healthcare spending in Chile now comes directly out of families' pockets — far above OECD averages and a sign that the insurance system is failing to cover real costs.

The survey data is striking: 84% of Chileans say they fear being unable to afford medical care not covered by their insurer — a concern that cuts across income levels and political leanings. The report attributes the out-of-pocket spiral partly to the waiting-list problem: when Fonasa queues are too long, patients pay privately to skip them, driving up household health debt. The CEP is calling for structural reform, not just more surgery funding.

Politics

Kast Meets with Renovación Nacional Tonight to Tighten Coalition's Legislative Grip

President Kast will sit down tonight with legislators from Renovación Nacional (RN) at his Cerro Castillo residence in Viña del Mar — the latest in a series of one-on-one presidential meetings with each party in his governing coalition. RN is Chile's center-right establishment party, roughly analogous to the pre-Trump mainstream Republican wing of Kast's coalition: more technocratic and less ideological than his own Republican Party. The meetings are designed to align the legislative agenda and smooth over persistent tensions between the coalition's hardline and moderate factions.

The round of encounters comes as the administration tries to shore up its legislative position with approval ratings at their lowest. Getting RN lawmakers committed to the agenda is essential for Kast to pass any major legislation — his coalition controls neither chamber outright and must negotiate with centrist parties on most bills.

Interesting & Offbeat

Chilean Rock's Greatest Hits — Now on Cassette

A new limited-edition tape series from Fusión Records and M&E Discos is putting five pillars of Chilean rock back on physical media: Los Prisioneros' foundational La Voz de los '80 (their era-defining 1984 debut — banned from radio under Pinochet for overtly political lyrics) and their double live Estadio Nacional; Pettinellis' self-titled debut (its first-ever cassette release); Los Tres' Unplugged 20 Años; and Los Miserables' La Voz del Pueblo. In the age of Spotify, it's the ultimate collector's item for anyone who grew up with these bands — and a reminder that Chilean rock of the '80s and '90s was a genuinely world-class scene that happened to be operating under a dictatorship.

"La Cancillería de Kast: Amistades Peligrosas"

International law attorney Paz Zárate argues Kast has abandoned Chile's 30-year tradition of nonpartisan "State Foreign Policy" in favor of pure ideological alignment with the global far-right. Her specific indictments: snubbing Brazil's President Lula at his inauguration (prioritizing Kast's relationship with Bolsonaro's sons over Chilean-Brazilian state relations), diplomatic cozying with Argentina's Milei, and leaving Chile exposed to a U.S. tariff threat — Washington added Chile to a list of 60 countries under investigation for a potential 12.5% surcharge on imports over labor standards. A recent consular scandal in Haiti, where irregularities in document legalization led to a diplomat's removal, rounds out what she calls a pattern of ideology over statecraft.

"¿Tiene claro la derecha que el fracaso puede estar a la vuelta de la esquina?"

In an unusually candid piece from a center-right publication, veteran analyst Muñoz Riveros asks bluntly whether Kast's governing coalition grasps the scale of its problems. With approval at 32%, economic growth near zero, and none of the major campaign promises delivered, he warns that what looks like a midterm slump could calcify into a permanent realignment — and that the right needs to pivot from ideological posturing toward practical governance fast. Think of it as the Chilean equivalent of a Never-Trumper warning the GOP that winning the argument doesn't mean winning the country.

Recent Releases & Scene
RockIndie

Los Tres — XCLNT

The marquee Chilean music release of 2026: XCLNT dropped in April — the first studio album from Los Tres with their original four-member lineup in 27 years. (Their last was Fome, 1999.) Recorded at Abbey Road Studios in London, the 11-track album revisits the band's signature blend of rock, blues, and cueca rhythms that made them the defining alternative act of 1990s Chile. Think of it as the Chilean equivalent of Wilco or R.E.M. getting the original band back together and actually making a great record. Singles "Cantar y Amar" and "Como Llegaste Te Vas" are streaming now on all platforms — for fans of rootsy, literate rock with Latin-folk underpinnings, this is essential listening.

El Mulu — Derramar el Tiempo

A quiet find from Santiago's independent circuit: El Mulu's debut album blends introspective folk songwriting with sparse, patient production — think a Chilean Elliott Smith crossed with the more reflective side of Calexico, with Spanish-language lyrics rooted in everyday melancholy. The project came to wider attention after being selected for Compilado Chile 2026, a curated compilation of 15 emerging Chilean independent acts drawn from an open call that attracted over 100 applicants. If you like stripped-back, lyric-driven music that doesn't need to fill the room, start here.
